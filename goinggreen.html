Going Green

Faster than you can say eco-chic, caring about the environment has gone from fringe to status symbol. Getting down to earth no longer means suffering for your cause. And with feel good websites popping up quicker than pesticide-free produce, it's obvious that there's more to greening your life than separating paper and plastic from your garbage.

 
  • Green Your Wardrobe
    www.thegreenloop.com

    Thankfully modern organic fibers don't mean scratchy hemp smocks anymore. online boutique, Greenloop carries sustainable men's and women's clothing lines that are far more hip than hippy.

     
  • Green Your Garden & Your Outdoors
    www.terracycle.net

    Even the greenest of thumbs could put up with a little crap from Mother Nature. TerraCycle's flagship planet food is created from worm poop and thriftily sold on recycled soda bottles. Nonprofit Leave No Trace encourages outdoors enthusiasts to do just that. The old leaving a place cleaner than you found it chestnut is just a starting point for lessening your impact on the wild.

     
  • Green Your Wedding
    www.terracycle.net

    Portovert is the magazine for eco-savvy brides and grooms. Get tips on socially responsible wedding ensembles, showers ceremonies, and honeymoons without sacrificing luxury.

     
  • Green Your Medicine Cabinet
    www.recycline.com

    It's one thing to recycle but making garbage into useful items is something else altogether. Recycline transforms Stonyfield Farm yogurt cups into colorful razors, toothbrushes and tongue cleaners that bear no resemblance to their original form.

     
  • Green Your Computer
    www.epeat.net

    Before you buy a computer use the Electronic Product Environmental Assessment Tool Devices are rated on criteria such as reduction of environmentally sensitive materials, energy conservation and packaging, and are awarded Gold, Silver and Bronze certificates based on their scores.

     
  • Green Your Home Decor
    www.homeandplanet.com

    Home and Planet claims to be the country's largest ecofurnishing store, and they cover all bases. Quirky items like symilk cartons crafted into coin urses share space with elegant reclaimed wood coffee tables.

     
  • Green Your Cleaning
    www.consumer.simplegreen.com

    Lackigng flashy packaging or exotic fragrances, non-toxic Simple Green has been around for over thirty years. The classic all-purpose cleaner is now just one product in a series for vehicles, home and hands.

     
  • Green Your Pet
    www.onlynaturalpet.com

    Due to the recent tainted pet food scare, organic kibble has become mainstream. From earth-friendly bedding to homeopathic remedies, Only Natural pet Store stocks everything a furry companion might need.

     
  • Green Your Junk
    www.freecycle.org

    One man's garbage being anothers treasure fuels giving and getting on Freecycle. Don't let your old George Foreman grill end up in a landfill when someone in your community is willing to take it off your hands.
